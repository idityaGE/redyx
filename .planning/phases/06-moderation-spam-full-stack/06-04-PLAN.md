**5. Update `internal/platform/auth/interceptor.go`:**
Review the publicMethods map. Most moderation/spam RPCs require auth (default). The only RPC that might need special handling:
- `CheckBan` and `CheckContent` are service-to-service calls — they come from post-service/comment-service, NOT from the frontend. These services connect directly to moderation/spam gRPC ports, bypassing Envoy, so the auth interceptor on those direct connections may need adjustment.
- Solution: Service-to-service calls use direct gRPC connections (not through Envoy) and may not have JWT tokens. Add these methods to publicMethods on the moderation and spam services since they're called internally:
  - `/redyx.moderation.v1.ModerationService/CheckBan` — called by post/comment services
  - `/redyx.spam.v1.SpamService/CheckContent` — called by post/comment services
  - `/redyx.spam.v1.SpamService/ReportSpam` — called by spam consumer
  - `/redyx.moderation.v1.ModerationService/SubmitReport` — called by spam consumer (service-to-service)

  But SubmitReport also needs to work from the frontend (user reports). When called from frontend it goes through Envoy with JWT. When called from spam consumer it's service-to-service without JWT. Solution: make the SubmitReport RPC extract claims optionally — if present, use claims.UserID as reporter_id; if not present (service call), require reporter_id in the request body.

  Actually, the simpler pattern used in this project: SubmitReport from frontend goes through Envoy with auth. The spam consumer calls a different internal version or passes its own system user. For simplicity: add SubmitReport to publicMethods (it checks reporter_id in the request body), and CheckBan/CheckContent are also public since they're internal-only.
